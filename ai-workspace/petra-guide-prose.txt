IMPORTANT: This generator is not suitable for explicit / sexual images. DALL - E content policy is quite sensitive, so you might run into issues depending on your prompt. Microsoft Bing / Microsoft Designer uses DALL - E for image generation; DALL - E is usually not free, but Microsoft offers quite a lot of usages per day and in my experience, while not as customizable as Perchance's, it yields fantastic results with prompts. You have 15 boosts per day, but the boosts only mean you can generate faster (even without boosts it's quite fast to generate). The true daily limit seems to be around 100 - 150 generations per account . If you have multiple accounts on Microsoft, each will have its own limit. Each generation tries to make 4 images, but generations may yield less than 4 images if it gets blocked by DALL - E's content policy. The prompt does not use Perchance's customizable format, and each prompt seems limited to 480 characters, so you have to be creative with this character limit. https://www.bing.com/images/create Things to keep in mind: You can ask to regenerate your prompt in landscape format by opening one image and clicking on resize. I'm able to stack up to 3 generations like this; it's tedious that you have to do this for every image, but that's what it is. • You can only browse some of your latest prompts, not all. Bing's collection feature does not store the image forever, so make sure to save the images to your PC or a cloud storage. • Microsoft Bing DALL - E domingo, 8 de setembro de 2024 03:12 Microsoft Bing DALL - E página 3

What is Perchance AI Character Chat (ACC)? Perchance is a website about various generators, some including AI. This guide is specifically for Perchance AI Character Chat , which essentially boils down to a tool that allows you to have an AI act as specific characters according to descriptions that are provided. How to write your lore entries As advised in the documentation , here are a few instructions about the lore entries: Lore entry order does not matter; the AI reads each lore entry individually, so the order you place them in the file has no effect. 1. Lore entries should be self - contained. The AI sees them in isolation. One lore entry cannot not reference another. You can sort of tie them by the same subject (just as I detailed "Amazon Empire" on all my lore entries on the previous guide). 2. Here is an example of good and bad written lore: --- Jonas Beltford is a police officer in New York with a few years of service under his belt. He is regarded as very professional and calm by his colleagues. He has a brother named Peter, who is a firefighter. --- The above two lore entries are bad, because it violates rule 2. The AI will see each lore entry in isolation, so it doesn't know that "He" refers to Jonas. To correct this, make sure to add Jonas' name so that the AI knows who "He" is. --- Jonas Beltford is a police officer in New York with a few years of service under his belt. He is regarded as very professional and calm by his colleagues. Jonas Beltford has a brother named Peter, who is a firefighter. --- The above lore entries are good, because now, the AI will be able to understand who has a brother named Peter. If you want to have lore entries that reference the same subject, make sure to have keywords that tie them together in some way (in this case, Jonas Beltford). At times, it's best to try to merge a few lore entries together so that if the AI retrieves it, it has more information to use.
