 3. Creating Your Character 
 
 Basic Character Profile 
 Start by filling out these essential fields in the Character Creation Panel: 
 
 
 Name: Your character's name (e.g., "Alex the Wizard") 
 Short Description: A brief summary of who your character is (e.g., "A 300-year-old wizard who specializes in time magic") 
 Personality: Key traits that define your character (e.g., "Wise, absent-minded, curious, and slightly mischievous") 
 Background: Your character's history and context (e.g., "Alex was born in medieval England but has lived through centuries using time magic. He has accumulated vast knowledge but tends to mix up historical events.") 
 
 
 Appearance & Voice 
 These details help shape how your character expresses themselves: 
 
 Physical Appearance: Describe how your character looks 
 Speaking Style: How your character talks (formal, casual, uses specific phrases) 
 Voice Characteristics: Tone, pitch, accent, or speech patterns 
 
 
 Knowledge & Abilities 
 Define what your character knows and can do: 
 
 Skills: What your character is good at 
 Knowledge Areas: Fields your character knows about 
 Limitations: Things your character doesn't know or can't do

 7. Tips & Best Practices 
 
 
 
 Be Specific 
 The more details you provide in your character profile, the more consistent and interesting their responses will be. Vague descriptions lead to generic characters. 
 
 
 
 Include Flaws 
 Perfect characters are boring! Give your character weaknesses, quirks, and limitations to make them more believable and engaging. 
 
 
 
 Test and Refine 
 Chat with your character and see how they respond. If something doesn't feel right, refine their profile and try again. 
 
 
 
 Use Speaking Style Examples 
 Include examples of how your character speaks in their profile. This helps the AI match their linguistic style consistently. 
 
 
 
 Troubleshooting Common Issues 
 
 Character is inconsistent: Add more specific details to your character profile and increase the consistency setting. 
 Responses are too generic: Your character description may be too vague. Add unique traits and specific examples of how they speak. 
 Character "breaks character": Include clear guidelines about what your character would never do or say. 
 Responses are too short/long: Adjust the response length setting or specify your preference in the character profile.
